Let me tell you about today.

I've built websites before. I know web dev. I have actual deployed projects that real humans use.

So when I woke up thinking "today I'll do the frontend," I genuinely believed it would take 2-3 hours. Maybe 4 if I'm slow.

It took the whole day.

---

THE FIGMA ARC:

First mistake: opened Figma.

I've never used Figma. I thought "how hard can it be, it's just shapes."

1 hour 10 minutes later I had a skeleton that looked like it was designed by someone who's only ever described websites to a sketch artist. Over the phone. In a different language.

That's when I realized: design is not my thing. Design has never been my thing. I just forgot because I always used templates.

Pivoted. Used AI to generate mockups. 25 minutes. 4 screens. Done.

Lesson: Leverage your tools. Stop making yourself suffer for no reason.

---

THE CSS/TEMPLATE ARC:

I know HTML/CSS. I've written it before. I've shipped projects with it.

But writing it from scratch? Clean? Matching a design?

My brain forgot how flexbox works. I'm not joking. I stared at `justify-content: center` like it was hieroglyphics.

Template errors everywhere. `{% include %}` is not `{% extends %}`. An hour of my life. Gone.

---

THE JAVASCRIPT ARC:

This is where it got philosophical.

I know JavaScript. I've used it in projects. I can READ it. I understand what it does.

Writing it from scratch?

Fighting Mahoraga from JJK.

On basics.

Every function I wrote felt like: "I know what this should do. Why is my brain empty. Why am I Googling 'how to add event listener.' I've done this. I'VE DONE THIS."

It's like eating a candy that looks sweet on the outside but the core is lava. Users enjoy the melted chocolate. Devs like me know the center is a hellhole.

---

THE REAL STORY:

I leaned on AI. Hard.

Prompt engineering got me through CSS. Got me through the boilerplate. Got me through the parts where my brain just... buffered.

But here's the thing: I still laid every brick. I chose what goes where. I understood what I was accepting. I debugged. I connected pieces. I made decisions.

AI is leverage. Not replacement. 

I was mid-build, mid-doubt, staring at code going "what the hell is this" â€” and I kept building anyway.

THE POINT:

I underestimated frontend. Simple as that.

Not because it's "hard" â€” but because writing it clean, from scratch, matching a design, while learning gaps you didn't know you had?

That's a different game.

Day 4 was a grind. It was messy. It was humbling.

And now it's done.
